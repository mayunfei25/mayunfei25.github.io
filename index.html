Academic Profile

Computational Urban Science, GeoAI, and Healthy Urban Environments.

Ph.D. Student, Institute of Future Human Habitats, Tsinghua Shenzhen International Graduate School

Computational Urban Science GeoAI Healthy Urban Environments

I am Yunfei Ma (马云飞), a Ph.D. student at the Institute of Future Human Habitats, Tsinghua Shenzhen International Graduate School. My academic training is rooted in urban and rural planning, with long-term interests in GIS, urban spatial analysis, and multi-source urban data.

My research focuses on the use of computational methods, GeoAI, and multi-source urban data in urban studies. I am interested in exploring the relationships among built environment characteristics, urban environmental quality, and residents' health.

My previous work includes identifying cognitive boundaries of urban business districts using toponym co-occurrence, assessing urban spatial performance using mobile phone signaling data, quantifying pedestrian systems using space syntax, and conducting disaster risk assessment with GIS.

Research experience

Urban perception, GeoAI, and healthy urban environments

Tsinghua SIGS · Ph.D. research · 2025–Present

My doctoral-stage research explores the use of satellite imagery, street-level imagery, GIS, and multi-source urban data for urban environmental sensing and built environment analysis. Current interests include urban spatial structure recognition, urban sound environment and environmental quality prediction, healthy urban environment assessment, and spatial impacts of emerging urban scenarios.

I also have exploratory exposure to low-altitude economy scenarios, including UAV operation contexts, UAV noise impacts, and spatial suitability analysis. This is treated as a related application context rather than the sole focus of my academic homepage.

Identifying cognitive regions of urban business districts using toponym co-occurrence

Harbin Institute of Technology, Shenzhen · 2022–2025

Proposed a framework for delineating the cognitive boundary of urban business districts using toponym co-occurrence from web pages, with methods including DBSCAN, KDE, fuzzy set analysis, and TF-IDF-based semantic interpretation.

Urban spatial performance assessment using mobile phone signaling data

Harbin Institute of Technology, Shenzhen · 2022–2025

Derived intra-urban spatial interaction intensity from mobile phone signaling trajectories, applied reverse gravity modeling and particle swarm optimization, and used random forest and SHAP to analyze factors associated with spatial performance differences.

Quantifying urban pedestrian systems using space syntax

Changsha University of Science and Technology · 2020–2022

Led a national undergraduate innovation project on pedestrian system analysis. Used DepthmapX to evaluate integration, choice, and intelligibility, and proposed pedestrian space improvement strategies based on field surveys.

GIS-based disaster risk analysis and assessment

Changsha University of Science and Technology · 2020–2021

Participated in disaster prevention and mitigation planning for Huayuan County, using ArcGIS and MATLAB to conduct entropy-weighted TOPSIS, AHP, and multi-model risk assessment.

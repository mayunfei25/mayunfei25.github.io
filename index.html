About me

I am Yunfei Ma (马云飞), a PhD student at Tsinghua University Shenzhen International Graduate School. My research lies at the intersection of urban science, GIS, urban big data analytics, social sensing, visual intelligence, and environmental AI.

My previous work focused on identifying vague cognitive regions of urban business districts using toponym co-occurrence from web pages, mobile phone signaling data, bike-sharing data, news reports, and spatial syntax. I am currently expanding my research toward visual urban sensing, satellite-based urban noise prediction, GeoAI, and hybrid quantum-classical machine learning for small-sample urban environmental prediction.

I aim to develop spatially aware, interpretable, and human-centered computational methods for understanding urban environments and supporting urban planning and governance.

Selected research projects

Identifying cognitive regions of urban business districts using toponym co-occurrence

Proposed a framework for delineating vague urban business district regions using toponym co-occurrence from web pages, with methods including DBSCAN, KDE, fuzzy set analysis, and TF-IDF-based semantic interpretation.

Urban space performance assessment using mobile phone signaling data

Used intra-urban spatial interaction intensity derived from mobile phone signaling trajectories to assess urban space performance, supported by reverse gravity modeling, random forest, and SHAP-based interpretation.

Catchment areas of bicycle-metro transfer trips using bike-sharing data

Investigated spatiotemporal characteristics of bicycle-metro transfer catchment areas around metro stations and explored factors influencing transfer trip density.

Association network and spatial interaction analysis based on news reports

Used text mining, jieba word segmentation, TF-IDF, ArcGIS, and Gephi to analyze Huaqiangbei’s influence from international, national, and local perspectives.

Satellite-based urban noise prediction and hybrid quantum-classical regression

Ongoing research on visual urban sensing, spatial validation, and fair benchmarking of hybrid quantum-classical regression methods for small-sample urban environmental prediction.

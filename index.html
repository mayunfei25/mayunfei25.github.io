About me

I am Yunfei Ma (马云飞), a PhD student at Tsinghua University Shenzhen International Graduate School. My research lies at the intersection of urban science, visual intelligence, remote sensing, GeoAI, and environmental modeling.

I am particularly interested in how satellite imagery, street-level visual data, and machine learning can help characterize urban environmental quality. My current work focuses on satellite-based urban noise prediction and the fair evaluation of hybrid quantum-classical learning methods on small-sample urban sensing tasks.

My broader goal is to develop computational tools that are technically rigorous, spatially aware, and useful for human-centered urban design and environmental decision-making.

Research Interests

  • Urban visual intelligence: learning urban morphology and environmental signals from remote sensing and street-level imagery.
  • Urban noise prediction: estimating sound environment indicators using visual, spatial, and geospatial data.
  • GeoAI and spatial validation: designing robust evaluation protocols for spatially dependent urban datasets.
  • Hybrid quantum-classical machine learning: evaluating quantum kernel methods such as QKRR and QSVR against matched classical baselines.
  • AI-assisted urban design: supporting noise mitigation and sustainable built environment planning.
